UCMC Website Redesign

After becoming a co-manager of the UC Men's Chorale in Fall 2014, I saw a need to update the website. As my very first choir conductor always said, "People hear with their eyes as much as their ears". If that's true then people had been hearing something that was ugly, outdated and not very user-friendly.

More importantly, the old site (click here to see it!) was written in straight html, and was therefore tedious to update. This site is built in Wordpress and, through use of a MySQL database, can be edited within the browser, no coding ability required!

UC Chorale Ensembles Website Implementation

In Fall 2014, I was tasked with not only a website redesign, but also the transfer of an entire organisation to a new (much better) interface for updating website content. The site needed to be easy to edit for managers of chorale groups, and also attractive and user-friendly. The site is a vital channel for donations to UCCE.

UC Chorale Ensembles is the parent company of the UC Men's Chorale and has more than thirty managers and directors with various and often conflicting personal interests. Despite these challenges, the resulting website is both beautiful and functional, making life better for UCCE and its audience.

Evernote Hackathon Design Prize

During our first ever hackathon, Tara Rezvani, Edwan Hernandez, Rongchang Lei and I wanted to add functionality to the Evernote platform that we would want to use ourselves.

Annot8 is an app built for those who need to take notes along to a lecture, interview, performance, or any other time-sensitive event. The functionality is simple: as you take your notes, each time you press enter, a timestamp with the specific minute of the recording is conveniently inserted just before your text. That way, when you review your notes, you can quickly access the moment in the recording to which your notes correspond.
